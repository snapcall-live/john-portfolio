Dental Sleep and DSO Target Profiles

Deeper account notes for the named partner universe. Scores are directional and based on public signals, not private claims.

How to use thisLead with the ranking logic in the interview, then use these profiles to show you can turn a strategy into an actual account list.

Sleep Better Georgia / Oral Appliance Therapy

Fit: dedicated sleep practice, Atlanta regional presence, and public claim that the practice is 100% focused on sleep medicine.

Best outreach: "OpenLoop can help you convert more physician and dental screening demand into at-home testing and faster interpretation without replacing your appliance workflow."

Why high priority: strong pilot site because the workflow is already clinically aligned and the practice can provide nuanced feedback.

Associates for Dental Sleep Medicine

Fit: strong authority signal from long experience and provider training/consulting positioning.

Best outreach: "We are building the infrastructure layer that can make dental sleep workflows easier for practices you already influence."

Why high priority: potential advisor, pilot partner, or center-of-influence channel.

Dental Sleep Solutions of Greenville

Fit: focused CPAP alternative / oral appliance practice with patient education and physician-prescribed treatment language.

Best outreach: "Happy Sleep plus OpenLoop can give your patients a simpler diagnostic bridge before appliance therapy."

Why high priority: likely understands treatment conversion economics and patient objections.

Sleep Better Illinois

Fit: strong education around oral appliance therapy, guidelines, and ongoing dentist/sleep specialist follow-up.

Best outreach: "We can help operationalize the guideline-based collaboration you already describe."

Why high priority: good benchmark for medically responsible partner messaging.

PDS Health

Fit: top DSO target because the public brand is already about integrated dental-medical care.

Best outreach: "Sleep apnea is one of the clearest proof points for why dental-medical integration matters."

Pilot design: 10 offices, hygiene screening, ring deployment, physician review, treatment routing, and location-level dashboard.

Heartland Dental

Fit: largest scale target and strong supported-doctor growth platform.

Best outreach: "A sleep screening service line can help supported doctors provide more comprehensive care while preserving clinical autonomy."

Risk: too large for first proof. Approach after a smaller DSO or regional practice pilot.

The Aspen Group / Aspen Dental

Fit: enormous consumer footprint and operational standardization.

Best outreach: "Sleep screening can extend the brand from oral care access into whole-person health."

Risk: needs a very simple front-office and hygiene workflow to avoid slowing throughput.

MB2 Dental

Fit: doctor-partnership model could allow optional service-line adoption by motivated practices.

Best outreach: "Let entrepreneurial partner doctors add a sleep program without building telehealth infrastructure."

Risk: less uniform rollout; better for pilot pods than enterprise mandate.
